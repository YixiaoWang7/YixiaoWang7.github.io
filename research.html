Path Planning for Force-controlled Robotic Grinding of Hub Surfaces
Master Thesis
Oct. 2020~Dec. 2020
Shanghai Jiao Tong University

Aiming at force-controlled grinding problems of automobile hubs, I segment the complex surface based on predetermined sweeping line mode. On each sub-surface, I establish the constrained nonlinear optimization problem with goals of maximizing the total grinding volume and minimizing the residual grinding volume according to the numerical model of contact force and material removal volume, and then plan the residence time of discrete points in each path and the distance between two adjacent parallel paths. Interval movements between the sub-surfaces when not grinding are optimized by solving the nonlinear integer optimization problem to reduce non-grinding time of the tool. Experiments show that proposed algorithm can generate a full coverage grinding path. At the same time, the grinding amount can reach our expectation, the residual grinding amount is relatively average, and the grinding efficiency is high.

Toolpath Planning through Reinforcement Learning in Additive Manufacturing
Master Thesis
Jan. 2020~Sept. 2020, Jan.2021 ~ Current
Northwestern University

Toolpath planning in additive manufacturing is complex, tough but vital for the product quality and manufacturing efficiency. This research is aimed at toolpath planning through reinforcement learning to explore whether artificial intelligence is capable to take a step in this area. First, this research focuses on manufacturing efficiency. Traditional coverage path planning algorithm is reproduced and imporved. Muzero, an advanced reinforcement learning network is utilized in this area and analyzed. Second, this research explores the capablity of Muzero in sparse reward structure, which denotes the product quality which is hard to model mathematically.

Simultaneous Localization and Mapping on the Mobile Robot
Course Project
Mar. 2020~June 2020
Northwestern University

Simultaneous localization and mapping is a hot research topic in robotics and covers various fields of knowledge. This research realized Extended-Kalman-Filter-based SLAM and valided it in Gazebo based on ROS. All the codes are written individually in C++ and no algorithm packages are called. Turtlebot3 is controlled by the computer through WIFI but SLAM is valided in simulation due to epidemics.

Path Planning for 6-DOF Robot Grasping in Obstacles
Bachelor Thesis
Dec. 2017~June 2018
Shanghai Jiao Tong University

The task of 6-DOF robot arm is to grasp different objects and carry them into correct boxes. This research is to plan a collision-free path for the robot arm. Rapid Random Tree (RRT) is one common approach to this problem. This research combined the prior characteristics of RRT-connect and RRT-star into RRT-cs, the proposed algorithm in this research , introduced heutristic function inspired by A* to direct growing directions of the tree, and enhanced the effectiveness of random sampling with random direction sampling or probability sampling field. The result shows RRT-cs exceeded other mentioned algorithms in overall performance. Random direction sampling and probability sampling field both strengthed the capability of RRT-cs in dense environment.
